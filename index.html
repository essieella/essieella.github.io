Union Market- favorite place to eat!

Credits: Washington.org


5 stars- food quality

I have never had any horribly tasting food there. The empanadas that I had in Union Market were scrumptious.

3 stars- affordability

The price is steep. 10 dollars for three empanadas is pricey. Even their vending machine products were expensive. The cookie butter, a small jar, was five dollars!

5 stars- appearance

The place inside and out is beautiful and is artfully designed.

IKEA sleepovers

IKEA sleepovers are a new dare campaign. It seems really neat, however it is illegal for a person to stay overnight in a locked store. According to an Ikea UK spokesperson: "We appreciate that people are interested in Ikea and want to create fun experiences, however the safety and security of our co-workers and customers is our highest priority and that's why we do not allow sleepovers in our stores." There are some legal store run sleepovers that I am researching. I would just love to stay in the one below. But, I don't think the sleepovers work like that.

Coding Classes

I just don't think it was worthwhile for me because I didn't need encouragement. In five weeks of classes, I learned two new things. Learning hangul

I am learning the Korean alphabet, Hangul. It is supposedly a very clever alphabet and the koreans are so proud of it that they have a day dedicated to celebrating it. I am using it to pronunce the labels on the Korean dumpling packet. It is actually quite easy. I've only started learning on Friday and I am getting quite good at it. If you want to learn, check out the websites in the description. Sorry that the visual is in french. It was the only one I found that could fit.

Math notes: 12.1

Polyhedrons- 3D bounded by concave polygons. Faces- enclose space. Edge- line segment formed by faces. Vertex- where 3+ faces meet. Regular- all are congruent regular polygons.

Platonic Solids

The first number is the number of faces. The second is vertices. The third is edges.
